1. Soak the corn husks in warm water for 30 minutes.
2. In a large skillet, cook the ground pork over medium-high heat until browned.
3. Add the chili pepper, tomato, onion, garlic, cumin, salt, pepper, oregano, cloves, allspice, and cinnamon to the pork and stir to combine.
4. Add the chicken broth and bring to a simmer.
5. To assemble the tamales, place a heaping spoonful of the pork mixture in the center of each corn husk.
6. Fold the husks up around the filling, tying them shut with string or strips of corn husk.
7. Place the tamales in a steamer basket and steam over boiling water for 1 hour.
8. Serve with your favorite toppings, such as sour cream or salsa.
